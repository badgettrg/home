Cholesterol and Benefit of Statins Facts1
What are statin drugs for? To reduce the chance of having and dying from a heart attack. The drug works by lowering cholesterol.
Who might consider taking statins? Men and women at risk of heart or vascular disease (stroke or narrowed blood vessels due to cholesterol build up)
Who should NOT take statins? People who have liver problems or heavy alcohol intake, women who are breastfeeding or who are pregnant, people who have myopathy
What do expert organizations recommend?

The guidelines by the American College of Cardiology and American Heart Association (ACC/AHA) recommend taking statin if your risk of cardiovascular disease (CVD) within 10 years is 7.5% or more or if you have diabetes or if you already have CVD.
Other things to consider doing and alternatives to statin therapy
  • Heart-healthy lifestyle: eating a diet rich in vegetables, fruits, and whole grains.
  • Getting regular exercise: check with your healthcare provider how often and how much is right for you.
  • Maintaining a healthy weight.
  • Not smoking or getting help quitting.
  • Eat a low saturated fat/low cholesterol diet, don't smoke, exercise more, look into other medications.
