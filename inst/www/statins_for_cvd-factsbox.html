Cholesterol and Benefit of Statins Facts
What are statin drugs for? To reduce the chance of having and dying from a heart attack. The drug works by lowering cholesterol.
Who might consider taking statins? Men and women at risk of heart or vascular disease (stroke or narrowed blood vessels due to cholesterol build up)
Who should NOT take statins? People who have liver problems or heavy alcohol intake, women who are breastfeeding or who are pregnant, people who have muscle disease

What do expert organizations recommend?

The guidelines by the USPSTFUnited States Preventive Services Task Force recommend that you take a statin if you are aged 40 to 75 years old and your risk of cardiovascular disease (CVD) within 10 years is 10% or more and you have either dyslipidemia (defined as an LDL–C level greater than 130 mg/dL or a HDL–C level less than 40 mg/dL), diabetes, hypertension, or smoking.
