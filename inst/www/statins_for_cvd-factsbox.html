Cholesterol and Benefit of Statins Facts
What are statin drugs for? To reduce the chance of having and dying from a heart attack. The drug works by lowering cholesterol.
Who might consider taking statins? Men and women at risk of heart or vascular disease (stroke or narrowed blood vessels due to cholesterol build up)
Who should NOT take statins? People who have liver problems or heavy alcohol intake, women who are breastfeeding or who are pregnant, people who have myopathy
What do expert organizations recommend?

The guidelines by the American College of Cardiology and American Heart Association (ACC/AHA) recommend taking statin if your risk of cardiovascular disease (CVD) within 10 years is 7.5% or more or if you have diabetes or if you already have CVD. (show)

"4 major statin benefit groups were identified for whom the ASCVD risk reduction clearly outweighs the risk of adverse events based on a strong body of evidence. These are 1) secondary prevention in individuals with clinical ASCVD, 2) primary prevention in individuals with primary elevations of LDL-C ≥190 mg/dL, 3) primary prevention in individuals with diabetes 40 to 75 years of age who have LDL-C 70 to 189 mg/dL, and 4) primary prevention in individual without diabetes and with estimated 10-year ASCVD risk ≥7.5%, 40 to 75 years of age who have LDL-C 70 to 189 mg/dL. Moderate evidence supports the use of statins for primary prevention in individuals with 5% to <7.5% 10-year ASCVD risk, 40 to 75 years of age with LDL-C 70 to 189 mg/dL. Selected individuals with <5% 10-year ASCVD risk, or <40 or >75 years of age may also benefit from statin therapy."(American College of Cardiology/American Heart Association, 2013 )
Other things to consider doing and alternatives to statin therapy
  • Heart-healthy lifestyle: eating a diet rich in vegetables, fruits, and whole grains.
  • Getting regular exercise: check with your healthcare provider how often and how much is right for you.
  • Maintaining a healthy weight.
  • Not smoking or getting help quitting.
  • Eat a low saturated fat/low cholesterol diet, don't smoke, exercise more, look into other medications.
