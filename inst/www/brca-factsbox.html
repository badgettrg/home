Breast cancer and mammography facts
What is this screening for? Detect breast cancer early to reduce the chance of dying from breast cancer.
Who should consider screening? For average risk women aged 40 to 74 years. Women over age 75 can consider screening if life expectancy is 10 years or longer.
How long has mammography been used? Since the 1960s
Who should NOT take the test? Women with a life expectancy of less than 10 years.
What do expert organizations recommend?

The United States Preventive Services Task Force "recommends screening every 2 years for women aged 50 to 74. The decision to start screening for women aged 40 to 49 should be an individual one."1,2

The American Cancer Society "recommends annual screening for women age 45 to 54 and screening every 2 years for women 55 and older. Screening should continue as long as their overall health is good and they have a life expectancy of 10 years or longer. Women aged 40-44 should have the opportunity to begin annual screening."3
