Prostate cancer and PSA facts
What is this screening for? Find prostate cancer at an early, potentially curable stage before it has spread beyond the prostate.
Who should consider screening? Men aged 50 to 69 years. Earlier (ages 40-45 years) in men at increased risk for prostate cancer (African American race and a first-degree relative diagnosed with prostate cancer before age of 65).
Who should NOT take the test? Men with a life expectancy of less than 10 years.
What do expert organizations recommend?

 • The American College of Physicians, The American Urological Association and The American Cancer Society recommend discussion of screening (references 1-3).
• The United States Preventive Services Task Force does not recommend screening (reference 4).3
Other things to consider doing Dietary changes may reduce risk (more information from cancer.gov).
